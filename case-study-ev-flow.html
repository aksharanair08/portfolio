EV-FLOW

App Design

Understanding Electric Vehicle Drivers' Behaviors and Preferences to Facilitate Charging Station Selection.

About the project

Over the last ten years, electric vehicles (EVs) experienced a dramatic surge in popularity due to a range of factors that piqued public interest. Despite their growing appeal, the limited availability of charging points in comparison to gas stations is a notable concern, leading to 'range anxiety,' the apprehension of batteries getting drained before finding a charging point. Our research to understand EV drivers' behaviors and preferences will allow us to reduce range anxiety and mental load, allowing for an easier selection process.

How Can We Facilitate Charging Station Selection to Decrease Charging Anxiety and Mental Load of Electric Vehicle Drivers?

Background and Information

In the current application market, there are multiple competitors that focus on facilitating charging station processes, for example: PlugShare, ChargePoint, and Electrify America.

Pros

  • ✓ In-App paying methods.
  • ✓ Detailed charger type information.
  • ✓ Up to date charging speed information.
  • ✓ Latest queuing availability.

Cons

  • • Not all charging station locations are accurately reported, leading to inconsistent availability.
  • • Added charging stations might be private, for example a company or a house, etc.
  • • Users constantly switch between different apps based on their location or vehicle compatibility.

Literature Review

Purpose: Understanding the behavior and decision-making process of EV drivers when selecting charging stations to develop a user-friendly charging station app [EV-Flow].

Literature Review Key Findings: Issues include range anxiety, inconvenient charging times, inaccurate app data, lack of real-time data, and diverse user interfaces.

Factors Influencing Selection: Service and practical concerns, perceived utility, and built environment (Wang et al., Guo et al.).

Decision-Making Steps: Checking the state of charge, considering location, availability, and cost, and completing the charging process.

Ideal App Features: Accessibility, real-time data, compatibility, cost information, and data consolidation. Enhancements: Personalization, parking availability, nearby amenities.

EV-Flow Prototype: Provides real-time information on station availability and plug types, featuring an automated charging station selection for extended trips.

Aim: Reduce cognitive load, address user issues, respond to unique EV driver's needs and improve the overall charging station selection process.

Sketching and Ideation

Ideation and Sketching Methods:

Round of Crazy 8's—a fast ideation method to create multiple screens and features.

Challenges:

How can we display availability of a charging station for quicker selection, rather than using reviews or ratings?

Features:

  • Map Home Screen: Display a map to display charging stations, and include a navigation bar for:
    • Profile: Change Name, Car Model, Plug Type, and Preferred Amenities.
    • History: A list of known saved stations.
    • Search Bar: A quick way to look up a specific charging station based on current location.
    • Plan Trip: A way to plan a road trip and get an automized route of charging stations based on their Car Model's average range.
  • Charging Station Details: Display the following details in this order:

    Charging Station Name, ETA, Distance Away, Address, Images, Open Hours, Charging Speed, Amenities, and Recent Reviews.

Prototype Introduction

We present you EV-Flow, an easy-to-use charging station application to help EV drivers find the best station and keep the energy flowing!

The interactive prototype, EV-Flow, aims to facilitate decision-making for electric vehicle (EV) drivers. Constructed using Figma software, this application is designed to help users locate the best charging station according to their needs.

After logging in or signing up, users are led to the core of the app, a map that indicates their location and nearby charging stations. EV-Flow is meant to be used for researchers to collect data on EV drivers' thought processes and decision-making factors when choosing a charging station.

Prototype Charging Station Details

EV-Flow focuses on displaying clear charging station availability and the types of plugs available in real-time.

For example, it shows that out of five available spots, four are occupied at the "EVgo Charging Station," and only one J-1772 plug is free for use. The intended goal is to simplify the decision-making process for Electric Vehicle (EV) drivers, through visually appealing representation of nearby amenities and charging costs.

EV-Flow also includes recent reviews of the charging station, as this value proposition, that relies on crowdsourced information, can influence a driver's decision and create relevancy about a specific station's status.

Eventually, such intricate details aim to gather more comprehensive data on what aspects EV drivers consider, possibly even unconsciously, when choosing a charging station—an aspect which may not have been fully elucidated in previous surveys.

Prototype Charging Station Details - Attention Analysis

To test attention levels being given to each charging station detail we used the software Attention Insight, which uses machine learning technology trained with 70,000+ images from real eye-tracking studies to test this specific screen and create a focus map.

Red Areas:

  • Title of the charging station
  • Availability bar showcasing the current charging station's availability
  • ETA of each charging station

This is a successful analysis since it supports our design system and provides a level of certainty about our application's success, as the most important details of the charging station's details are receiving the most focus.

The Attention Insight software also analyzed our screen against multiple other images and determined that our design implementation lies under a 71% optimal clarity ranking, making our design clearer than 57% of the most popular websites.

Based on this report, we are hoping for a successful round of usability testing interviews to gather further quantitative data on the success of our application and proceed with future improvements on aspects that might be confusing.

Design Heuristics

Implemented design heuristics by Jakob Nielsen's 10 general principles for interaction design:

Visibility of System Status

Displayed charging stations based on availability, and with different colors to signify different levels of availability.

  • Red for busy stations
  • Yellow for semi-busy stations
  • Green for quiet or free stations

Consistency and standards

Employed standardized vocabulary by EV drivers and what is currently used in the EV industry, for example:

  • Plug type models
  • Charging cost information
  • Charging speed units and information

Safety Measures

Implemented a lock screen activated during driving to reduce distractions for users who might try to operate the application while driving, helping keep their focus on the road.

Usability Testing

During the Zoom usability testing sessions, participants were sent a Figma prototype link to EV-Flow and were invited to share their screens for a thorough evaluation of the app experience.

Usability testing was conducted using both in-person and online participants. In-person participants completed tasks using an iOS MacBook interface (EV-Flow via Figma) and iPhone interface (PlugShare via native app). Online participants used the same device combination, facilitated through Zoom screen sharing.

These sessions generated rich qualitative and quantitative data that will guide future iterations of EV-Flow, helping to further facilitate charging station selection for EV drivers.

Usability Tasks

Task 1

Objective

Select a nearby charging station.

Question

How easy or challenging was it to locate the search feature and interpret the results?

Metric

Time from the beginning of the task to the "Congratulations" screen, then compared between EV-Flow and competitor app PlugShare.

Task 2

Objective

Choose a charging station in Detroit.

Question

What specific information influenced your decision in selecting that particular charging station?

Metric

Rating, on a 1–5 scale, of which factor most influenced the decision (1 = Least important, 5 = Most important).

Task 3

Objective

Provide feedback or a rating for a recently visited charging station.

Focus

Capture open-ended feedback on clarity, visual hierarchy, and missing features within the station details screen.

Usability Testing Feedback

Iterations and Changes

Based on user feedback, we identified key opportunities to refine EV-Flow and better align it with EV drivers’ priorities.

  • Filter System: Initially overlooked due to profile preferences (plug type, amenities) acting as a proxy filter. Users highlighted a desire for an explicit filter to prioritize what matters most in the moment (e.g., cost, speed, amenities).
  • Search Bar: Originally implemented as a visual placeholder. Participants expected to search by city or station name (e.g., “Detroit”), which will be prioritized in future iterations.
  • Brightness & Color Vibrancy: The darker map was intended to increase contrast but in practice reduced clarity. We increased overall brightness and color vibrancy so charging icons stand out more, with a future opportunity to add a day/night mode toggle.

Before

After

The revised interface increases legibility and visual contrast where it matters most—charging availability and cost—while keeping room for future enhancements such as dynamic light/dark modes.
