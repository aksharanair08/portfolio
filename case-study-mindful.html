Seamount Fishes

Redesigning Seamount Fishes — the world beneath the waves.

An innovative mobile application designed to simplify and enhance the process of identifying reef fishes found around India's seamounts. In a field where accuracy, accessibility, and engagement are crucial, this redesign project sought to transform the user experience of the Seamount Fishes App — making it more intuitive, educational, and delightful for marine researchers, divers, and ocean enthusiasts.

About the App

Seamount Fishes App is an interactive guide for identifying reef fishes in the Indian Ocean. Designed for researchers, students, divers, and enthusiasts, it combines scientific accuracy with intuitive design.

Users can identify species in real time, explore a rich database with images and details, and access trusted resources like FishBase and the IUCN Red List. The app works online and offline, making research and exploration seamless.

By merging technology with marine science, it's more than a guide—it's an educational tool that sparks curiosity and awareness about marine life.

The main goal of this project was to reimagine the app into a powerful, easy-to-use digital companion that empowers users to explore India's rich marine biodiversity — both online and offline.

Key Features

  • AI-Based Fish Identification: Capture fish photos in real-time and identify species using an inbuilt AI trained on existing databases.
  • Offline Database Access: Identify fishes and access species data even without an internet connection.
  • Ask AI Assistant: Get quick, field-based support for uncertain fish observations.
  • Scientific Resource Integration: Direct links to FishBase and IUCN Red List provide instant access to reliable external data.
  • Modern, Intuitive Interface: A clean, ocean-inspired interface designed for both beginners and experts.

User Research

Before beginning the redesign, a thorough user research phase was conducted to understand the needs, challenges, and goals of the target audience. The objective was to uncover usability gaps and ensure the new design aligned with real-world usage in marine environments.

Research Methods

  • User Interviews: Conducted with marine biologists, research students, and diving instructors to learn about current workflows and frustrations.
  • Contextual Inquiry: Observed how users interact with the app in the field, particularly in low-connectivity environments.
  • Surveys: Distributed to marine enthusiasts and students to understand their expectations from a fish identification tool.
  • Heuristic Evaluation: Reviewed the existing app to identify issues in navigation, visual hierarchy, and interaction design.

Key Insights

  • Users often face poor visibility and limited connectivity while identifying species underwater or in coastal areas.
  • The existing app lacked visual clarity and consistent navigation, leading to confusion in species search.
  • Users wanted a faster AI-assisted identification tool and simplified species comparison.
  • Researchers needed quick access to scientific sources and the ability to store and share offline data for collaboration.
  • Research students and enthusiasts desired a more visually appealing interface with simplified terminology.

Design System

Design Goals

Based on user insights, the redesign focused on the following principles:

Intuitiveness

Simplify the user flow for species identification and search.

Clarity

Use clear visual hierarchies and color-coded species indicators.

Accessibility

Ensure readability and usability in outdoor/underwater lighting.

Offline Reliability

Enable full functionality without internet dependency.

Scientific Trust

Maintain data integrity by linking with verified databases.

Engagement

Create a visually immersive experience that reflects oceanic life.

Design Journey

a)

Information Architecture

Reorganized the app structure to prioritize:

  • Quick identification
  • Easy species browsing
  • Seamless access to AI, search, and offline data
  • A simplified bottom navigation bar replaced nested menus for faster interaction.
b)

User Flow Development

Created detailed task flows for:

  • Capturing a fish photo → Identifying species → Viewing detailed info
  • Searching fish by color, shape, or region
c)

Wireframing

Low-fidelity wireframes mapped key journeys such as:

  • Home (Explore Species)
  • AI Identification
  • Species Details Page
  • Offline Database
d)

Visual Redesign

Adopted an oceanic theme inspired by underwater gradients and natural blues. Icons were made bold and intuitive; typography emphasized readability.

Each fish entry now features:

  • Species image
  • Common & scientific name
  • Identification markers (fin shape, color patterns, habitat)
  • Quick access buttons to FishBase/IUCN.
e)

Usability Testing

Conducted A/B testing with 8 participants from the research community.

  • 90% found the new navigation faster to use.
  • 70% requested a "Compare Two Species" option (added post-feedback).

Final Outcome

The redesigned Seamount Fishes App offers a cohesive and user-friendly experience tailored to real-world marine contexts. It merges scientific depth with intuitive design, enabling users to identify, learn, and share fish species effortlessly.

Key improvements include:

  • Reduced identification time by 40% with streamlined AI workflow.
  • Improved data visibility through better layout and typography.
  • Enhanced offline usability and faster navigation across all features.
  • Visually engaging design reflecting the serenity and vibrancy of underwater life.
